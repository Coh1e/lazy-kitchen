# French Onion Soup

<span class="status status-proposed">Proposed Recipe</span>

French Onion Soup is a cornerstone of French cuisine, renowned for its profound sweetness from caramelized onions, rich beef broth base, and the iconic gratinéed cheese crouton on top. It is a dish that demands patience and precision, but its ultimate reward is unparalleled comfort and elegance. This recipe aims to recreate the classic, emphasizing ingredient quality and meticulous cooking technique.

## Select Ingredients

- Yellow Onions: 1000 g (≈4-5 medium), finely sliced
- Unsalted Butter: 50 g
- Extra Virgin Olive Oil: 15 ml
- Garlic: 2 cloves, minced
- Dry White Wine: 250 ml (e.g., Sauvignon Blanc or Chardonnay)
- Quality Beef Stock: 1.5 L (refer to `SKU-BEEF-STOCK-001`)
- Bay Leaf: 1 piece
- Fresh Thyme Sprigs: 3-4
- Kosher Salt: to taste (for seasoning)
- Freshly Ground Black Pepper: to taste (for seasoning)
- Baguette: 4 slices (approx. 1.5 cm thick)
- Gruyère Cheese: 100 g, grated (refer to `SKU-GRUYERE-CHEESE-001`)

## Cooking Steps

1.  **Mise en place**: Peel and thinly slice onions, mince garlic, grate cheese, and slice the baguette. Prepare all ingredients and arrange them neatly.
2.  **Sweat & Caramelize**: In a heavy-bottomed Dutch oven or stockpot, melt butter over medium-low heat, then add olive oil. Add the sliced onions and a pinch of salt to help draw out moisture. Cook patiently, stirring occasionally, for about 30-40 minutes. The onions should slowly and evenly soften and turn a deep brown color. This is the result of the Maillard reaction, which gives the soup its characteristic sweetness and complex flavor. Do not burn them.
3.  **Deglaze**: Once the onions have reached their ideal caramelized color, add minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Pour in the dry white wine, using a wooden spoon to scrape up all the caramelized bits from the bottom of the pan. Bring the wine to a boil, allow the alcohol to evaporate, and reduce for approximately 2-3 minutes.
4.  **Simmer**: Add the beef stock, bay leaf, and fresh thyme sprigs. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for at least 20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld thoroughly.
5.  **Season & Prepare Croûtons**: Remove the bay leaf and thyme sprigs. Adjust the soup's seasoning with salt and black pepper until perfectly balanced. Simultaneously, preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F) and toast the baguette slices for 5-7 minutes until golden and crisp, or use `SOP-PAN-TOAST-001` to pan-fry until golden.
6.  **Gratiné**: Ladle the hot soup into oven-safe bowls. Place a toasted baguette slice on top of each bowl, then generously cover with grated Gruyère cheese. Place the bowls under a preheated broiler or in an oven at 200°C (400°F) for 5-8 minutes, or until the cheese is fully melted, bubbly, and golden brown. Be careful not to burn the cheese.
7.  **Serve**: Carefully remove the gratinéed soup bowls and serve immediately. Be aware that the bowls and soup will be very hot.
