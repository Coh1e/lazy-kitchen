# Food safety

LK's stance: **state risks clearly, don't pretend to be a sterile lab**.

## Key risk points

### Vacuum bags & sealers
- Must be **BPA-free, food-grade**
- Used packaging **cannot be re-sealed** (cross-contamination)
- Cannot microwave (high-temp leaching)
- Can freeze + boil (check temp rating)

### Vacuum-frozen meat
- Recommend -18°C or below
- Flavor window: 6–12 months; safety window: 24+ months
- Thaw in fridge overnight > running water > microwave (in order)
- **Do not refreeze after thawing**

### WET SKUs
- Sauces with fresh garlic / scallion: **must freeze**, fridge ≤ 3 days
- High-acid sauces (vinaigrette / nuoc cham): fridge 2 weeks
- Anhydrous oil-based (chimichurri): fridge 2 weeks

### COOKED SKUs
- Cool completely (≤ 2 hr to room temp) → vacuum → freeze
- Don't bag while hot (condensation accelerates spoilage)
- Thaw only what you'll use within a week

### Kitchen environment
- Cutting boards: separate raw / cooked (recommend 2 colored boards)
- Wash knife immediately after raw meat
- Replace dishcloth daily (mold breeding ground)

### Spices
- Whole spices: 3-year flavor window if sealed dark
- Ground spices: 6–12 months before going off
- Caked from moisture → discard (mold risk)

## Labeling

Frozen packs must show:
```
[name]  [date]  [batch]  [use-by]
```

See SOP-LABELING-001 (referenced when add-dish runs).

## When in doubt

- Bloated vacuum pack → discard immediately (anaerobic bacteria)
- Off smell / discoloration → discard
- Uncertain → discard is cheaper than testing yourself

LK does not assume food safety responsibility for you.
